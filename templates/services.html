Ultrasonic Pulse Velocity

Latest technology for investigating hardened concrete quality on-site. Measures ultrasonic pulse travel time — higher velocity indicates denser, uniform concrete with better strength. Also detects honeycombs, cold-joints, cracks, and discontinuities.

  • Concrete quality mapping
  • Defect identification
  • Strength correlation

Steel Jacketing

Structural strengthening by encasing elements with precision steel plates. Reduces required concrete section enlargement while providing significant strength gains.

RCD
{%- endif %}

Concrete Enlargement

Encasing structural elements with additional concrete — the most cost-effective method. Best applied where headroom and space constraints are not a primary concern.

{% if svc_images.get("rcd-support") %} {%- else %}
RCD
{%- endif %}

Additional Support

Adding structural members such as columns, beams, and supports. Structural steel is the preferred material for this approach due to its high strength-to-weight ratio.

Advanced Technologies
{% if svc_images.get("rcd-bracing") %} {%- else %}
RCD
{%- endif %}

Buckling Restrained Bracing

Seismic upgrade bracing system designed to allow buildings to withstand cyclical lateral loadings — particularly earthquake-induced loading — through engineered brace combinations.

{% if svc_images.get("rcd-carbonplate") %} {%- else %}
RCD
{%- endif %}

Pre-Stressed Carbon Plate

Latest method (2020) for flexural strengthening of long-span girders and beams. Derived from external post-tensioning principles — anchored at one end, tensioned at the other for maximum performance.

  • Long-span beam strengthening
  • Bridge girder applications
  • Minimal section increase
{% if svc_images.get("rcd-frp") %} {%- else %}
RCD
{%- endif %}

Fiber Reinforced Polymer

The latest technology for structural strengthening — FRP is many times stronger than steel at a fraction of the weight. First introduced in Japan in the 1980s; ACI-approved guidelines adopted in the late 1990s.

  • Easy to install in tight spaces
  • Lightweight, high tensile strength
  • Carbon fiber wrapping systems

Structural Cracks

Crack Repair Systems

Cracks in concrete are inevitable — but not all require the same treatment. Our engineers select the most appropriate method from routing & sealing, gravity application, high-pressure, and low-pressure injection to achieve an economical, lasting repair.

  • Routing & sealing for surface cracks
  • Pressurized epoxy injection
  • Gravity method for deep penetration
  • Low-pressure polyurethane grouting

Concrete Voids & Honeycombs

Concrete defects resulting in section loss are rectified through multiple methods: hand patch, chip and patch, form and pour, or shotcrete. The engineer selects the best approach based on defect type, location, and structural requirements.
